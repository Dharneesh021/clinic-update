Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of hearing loss include difficulty understanding conversations, especially in noisy environments or over the phone. You may frequently ask others to repeat themselves or find that people sound like they are mumbling. If you’re turning up the TV or radio volume higher than others prefer, that could also be an indication. Some individuals may also experience a ringing or buzzing sound in the ears (tinnitus).

You should consider a hearing test if you’ve noticed any of the signs mentioned above or if you feel your hearing isn’t as sharp as it used to be. People often delay testing, but early detection can make a big difference in treatment. If you’re over 50, it’s a good idea to have your hearing checked regularly as part of routine health care.

Hearing aids typically last 3 to 7 years, depending on the type and how well they are maintained. Regular cleaning and occasional servicing can extend their lifespan. Advances in technology also mean you may want to upgrade earlier to benefit from new features.

To prevent hearing loss, avoid prolonged exposure to loud noises, use ear protection, and regularly check your hearing health. Early intervention and treatment can help preserve your hearing.

Hearing loss can be hereditary, especially in cases of genetic conditions. However, factors like aging, noise exposure, and certain health issues can also contribute to hearing loss. If you have a family history of hearing issues, regular check-ups are recommended.
